Describing with Adjectives

Learn how adjectives describe nouns and make sentences more interesting.

What are Adjectives?

Adjectives are words that describe or give more information about nouns. They help us understand what something looks like, feels like, sounds like, or what kind of thing it is.

Think of adjectives as "describing words" that make our sentences more interesting and help others understand exactly what we're talking about.

Examples

• The big dog barked loudly.

• She wore a beautiful dress to the party.

• The cold water felt refreshing.

• We live in a small house.

Where Do Adjectives Go?

In English, adjectives usually come before the noun they describe.

Correct Examples

• The red car is fast.

• I have a new book.

• She is a kind teacher.

• We saw a funny movie.

Key Points to Remember

  • Adjectives describe nouns
  • They usually come before the noun
  • Adjectives can describe size, color, shape, feelings, and more
  • Using adjectives makes your writing more interesting
